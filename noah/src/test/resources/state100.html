JAPANESE ENCEPHALITIS - INDIA: (ASSAM) FATAL CASES
**************************************************
A ProMED-mail post
http://www.promedmail.org
ProMED-mail is a program of the
International Society for Infectious Diseases
http://www.isid.org

Date: Fri 17 Jun 2016
Source: The Northeast Today (TNT) [edited]
http://thenortheasttoday.com/japanese-encephalitis-and-dengue-claim-6-leave-200-affected/


Japanese encephalitis seems to have taken an even more serious turn at Lahorighat and Bhurbandha blocks of Morigaon district [Assam state], claiming 6 lives and affecting more than 200 people of several villages so far. As per a statement given by Dr. Biplab Kumar Gohain, Joint Director of Health, Morigaon, 4 such death cases have occurred due to Japanese encephalitis and dengue in the district in the past 48 hours. It was informed that at Lahorighat Tengaguri village, a 65-year-old female and a 9-year-old boy died of the disease. Their death reports confirmed infection by the acute encephalitis virus.

A 55-year-old female of Balijan village and another female of Solmari village have also lost their lives to this disease. As per a confirmed report of the medical team, a female of Borchapori village and 65-year-old lady of Lochanbori village also lost their lives to encephalitis and dengue, respectively. Meanwhile, measures are on the rise to contain the disease in the remote areas of the district, including awareness programmes and vaccination drives among others.

--
Communicated by:
ProMED-SoAs
<promed-SoAs@promedmail.org>

[As per the news report above, 5 persons have lost their lives due to viral encephalitis and 1 patient died due to dengue fever in different villages and blocks of Morigaon district of Assam state of India. More than 200 cases have been reported in different villages of the state so far. As such the report does not mention the exact etiology of the encephalitis cases; however, earlier reports from the northeastern India region had been due to Japanese encephalitis (JE) virus infections and to generic acute encephalitis (see ProMED-SoAs post 20151129.3826257). Simultaneously, a fatal case of dengue fever has also been reported from the region. In order to put in place effective measures for the prevention and control of both of the diseases, surveillance and laboratory diagnostic facilities should be strengthened to capture the cases for a timely response and monitoring of the disease trends. The authorities have already implemented awareness and immunization campaigns against JE in the area. - Mod.MS
